## üéØ Project Management Process Overview

### Core Principles

Our project management approach is built on five foundational principles:

- **Customer-first**: Prioritize customer value and usability in every decision
- **Iterative delivery**: Ship small, testable increments to learn and adapt quickly
- **Clear ownership**: Every project has a named Project Manager and Product Lead
- **Data-informed decisions**: Measure impact and iterate based on evidence
- **Psychological safety**: Encourage feedback, experimentation, and learning

### Key Roles

- **Project Manager (PM)**: Coordinates delivery, schedules, risk management, and communications
- **Product Manager (PdM)**: Defines outcomes, prioritizes backlog, and measures success
- **Developers**: Implement features, collaborate on design, and ensure testability
- **QA/Testing**: Validate quality standards and acceptance criteria
- **Stakeholders**: Provide inputs, feedback, and approvals

> üìñ For detailed role descriptions and responsibilities, see [OctoAcme Roles and Personas](octoacme-roles-and-personas.md)

### Project Lifecycle Phases

#### 1. üöÄ Initiation
The first phase focuses on validating the problem and aligning stakeholders before committing resources.

- Problem validation and business need confirmation
- Stakeholder identification and alignment
- Creation of Project One-pager with problem statement and goals
- Success metrics definition
- Initial timeline and resource estimation
- Go/no-go decision gate

#### 2. üìã Planning
Turn approved initiatives into actionable plans with clear delivery roadmaps.

- Backlog creation with prioritized, estimated work items
- Sprint/iteration planning with defined capacity
- Risk identification and dependency mapping
- Release planning with milestone definitions
- Definition of Done (DoD) establishment
- Test and QA approach planning

#### 3. ‚öôÔ∏è Execution & Tracking
Manage day-to-day delivery with consistent rhythm and quality standards.

- **Daily standups**: 15-minute check-ins focused on progress and blockers
- **Pull Request workflows**: Small PRs with clear acceptance criteria, automated testing, and peer review
- **Quality assurance**: Unit tests, integration tests, and security scanning
- **Metrics tracking**: Monitor velocity, burndown, and success metrics
- **Project board management**: Track work through Backlog ‚Üí Ready ‚Üí In Progress ‚Üí In Review ‚Üí QA ‚Üí Done

#### 4. üö¢ Release & Deployment
Deploy features to production with risk mitigation and observability.

- **Release types**: Patch (hotfixes), Minor (incremental features), Major (significant changes)
- **Pre-release requirements**: All acceptance criteria met, passing CI/security scans, release notes drafted
- **Deployment checklist**: Staging validation, smoke tests, production deployment, post-deploy verification
- **Rollback procedures**: Incident response and rollback to last known-good release if needed

#### 5. üîÑ Retrospective
Capture learnings and convert them into actionable improvements.

- What went well / what could be improved
- Action item identification with clear owners
- Follow-up on previous retrospective commitments
- Continuous improvement culture

### Communication Cadence

- **Weekly syncs**: PM + Product Manager alignment
- **Daily standups**: Delivery team (15 minutes)
- **Twice-weekly standups**: As agreed by team
- **Monthly stakeholder updates**: Broader organizational communication
- **Ad-hoc escalations**: As needed for critical issues

### Risk Management

- **Risk Register**: Maintained with ID, description, impact, likelihood, owner, and mitigation plan
- **Escalation paths**: Team ‚Üí PM ‚Üí Product Lead ‚Üí Sponsor
- **Mitigation strategies**: Proactive identification, assessment, and continuous monitoring
- **Weekly reviews**: Risk status updates in team syncs

## üöÄ Getting Started

### For New Team Members

1. **Start here**: Read this README to understand our overall approach
2. **Review your role**: Read the [Roles & Personas](octoacme-roles-and-personas.md) document to understand your responsibilities
3. **Understand the lifecycle**: Browse the process documents relevant to your current project phase
4. **Find your project**: Locate your project's Charter or One-pager in the project repository
5. **Join the rhythm**: Connect with your PM to understand team cadence and upcoming meetings
6. **Ask questions**: Use team channels or reach out to your PM for clarification

### For Project Managers

1. **Initiation**: Use the [Project Initiation](octoacme-project-initiation.md) guide to create your Project One-pager
2. **Planning**: Follow the [Project Planning](octoacme-project-planning.md) process to build your backlog and release plan
3. **Execution**: Reference [Execution & Tracking](octoacme-execution-and-tracking.md) for daily delivery management
4. **Release**: Use the [Release & Deployment](octoacme-release-and-deployment.md) checklist when shipping
5. **Retrospective**: Apply the [Retrospective](octoacme-retrospective-and-continuous-improvement.md) framework to capture learnings

### For Developers

1. **Understand the context**: Review your project's One-pager and backlog
2. **Follow workflows**: Check [Execution & Tracking](octoacme-execution-and-tracking.md) for PR and testing guidelines
3. **Communicate proactively**: Participate in standups and flag blockers early
4. **Maintain quality**: Write tests, document your code, and follow the Definition of Done

### For Product Managers

1. **Define outcomes**: Use the Project One-pager template from [Project Initiation](octoacme-project-initiation.md)
2. **Prioritize effectively**: Reference [Project Planning](octoacme-project-planning.md) for backlog management
3. **Track success**: Monitor the metrics defined in your Project Charter
4. **Stay aligned**: Maintain weekly sync with your PM and delivery team

### How to Use These Documents Effectively

- **Keep artifacts updated**: Maintain your Project Charter and risk register in your project repository
- **Integrate with Copilot**: Add relevant process docs to `.copilot/` for AI-assisted guidance
- **Adapt as needed**: These are guidelines, not rigid rules‚Äîadjust for your project's needs
- **Share knowledge**: Reference these docs in onboarding and team discussions

## üì¶ Key Artifacts

Throughout the project lifecycle, teams create and maintain these essential artifacts:

- **Project Charter / One-pager**: Problem statement, goals, success metrics, stakeholders
- **Roadmap and Release Plan**: Timeline, milestones, and delivery phases
- **Sprint/Iteration Backlog**: Prioritized work items with acceptance criteria
- **Acceptance Criteria & Definition of Done**: Quality standards and completion criteria
- **Risk Register**: Identified risks with impact, likelihood, and mitigation plans
- **Retrospective Notes and Action Items**: Learnings and improvement commitments
- **Release Notes**: Changes, migrations, and known issues for each release
- **Stakeholder Engagement Log**: Record of all stakeholder interactions and communication (use [template](stakeholder-engagement-log.md))
- **Action Item Tracking**: Follow-up on retrospective commitments (use [checklist](action-item-follow-up-checklist.md))
